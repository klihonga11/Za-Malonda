2.2.1 Introduction

Single entry bookkeeping is good for businesses that are very small and deal with a small number of cash transactions every month.

When recording the transaction, only one entry is recorded as either positive (money coming into the business) or negative (money going out of the business).

An example of a positive transaction would be receiving money from selling a product while a negative transaction would be losing money when paying the rent.

As a result, single entry bookkeeping can be used to calculate net income at the end of a period.

Net income is the total money that came into the business minus the total money that went out of the business over a period of time.
