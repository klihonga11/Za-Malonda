6.1 Introduction

The statement of profit or loss (also known as an income statement) is a financial statement that shows whether the business has made a profit or a loss at the end of the fiscal period.

A financial statement is a report that is made to show how well the business is doing.

The numbers used in a statement of profit or loss are taken from the trial balance.

There are two types of income:

  • Capital income: money invested by the owner of the business and loans from third parties. For example, the money Thomas used to start the business.
  • Revenue income: money made by the business from sales. The money Thomas receives from selling sugarcane.

There are two types of expenses:

  • Capital expenditure: money spent on items, which the business will benefit for more than one fiscal period. For example, the bucket Thomas will use to store sugarcane.
  • Revenue expenditure: money spent on day-to-day expenses. For example, the transport money used to buy sugarcane.

The profit or loss is calcluated by taking the revenue income minus the revenue expenditure.
