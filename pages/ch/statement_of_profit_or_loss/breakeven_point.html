6.7 Breakeven Point

When a new business starts, it begins operating at a loss. It will have spent money on inventory without making any sales.

As the business starts to make sales, it will begin getting closer to making a profit. But, it might still make a loss because the number of products that it sold did not bring in enough money to pay for its expenses.

The breakeven point is the number of products a business needs to sell to neither make a profit or a loss.

It is the point where revenue income equals revenue expenditure and the net profit equals 0.

Selling one more product at this point will result in making a profit while selling one less product will result in a loss.

Thomas bought sugarcane at K 100 and sells it at K 150. He needed to pay K 500 in transport to buy the sugarcane. What is his breakeven point?

Thomas needs to sell 10 sugarcane in order to breakeven.

If he sells 9 sugarcane, he will make K 450 in sales. His expenses will be K 500. Therefore, he will make a loss of K 50.

If he sells 11 sugarcane, he will make K 550 in sales. His expenses will be K 500. Therefore, he will make a profit of K 50.

The breakeven point is very important because it gives the business a sales target to aim for in order to start being profitable.
