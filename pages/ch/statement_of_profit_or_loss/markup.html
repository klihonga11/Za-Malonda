6.5 Markup

Markup is the amount added on top of the cost price to reach the selling price of a product.

Markup = selling price – cost price

However, when talking about a markup, it usually refers to the markup as a percentage.

Markup Percentage = (selling price – cost price) / cost price * 100

Thomas buys one sugarcane at K 100 and sells it at K 150. Using the formula, the markup on the sugarcane is K 50 which is a markup percentage of 50%. This means that the sugarcane was sold for 50% more than what Thomas paid for it.

If Thomas buys one sugarcane at K 100 but sells it at K 200, the markup on the sugarcane is 100% (K 100). This means that the sugarcane was sold for 100% more than what Thomas paid for it.
