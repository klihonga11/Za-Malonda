7.8 Bad Debts

A bad debt happens when a customer who owes money to the business for products they bought on credit becomes unable to pay the amount owed.

At that time, it should no longer be included in the account receivables figure as it is no longer an asset.

A debt should not be written off as a bad debt until the business is sure that they will never get the money back.

In order to account for a bad debt, it is necessary to:

  • Show the total bad debts written off in the fiscal period as an expense in the statement of profit or loss.
  • Subtract the amount of the bad debt from the accounts receivables figure in the statement of financial position.
