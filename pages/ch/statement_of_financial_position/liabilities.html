7.5 Liabilities

A liability is an amount of money the business owes that needs to be paid. For example, Thomas needs to pay back the K 50,000 loan.

Non-current liabilities refers to money that the business owes that will continue to be paid for after the current fiscal period. For example, long term loans.

Current liabilities refers to money the business owes to be paid for within the current fiscal period. For example, money owed to suppliers for purchases made on credit.

Both non-current and current liabilities can be listed in any order.
