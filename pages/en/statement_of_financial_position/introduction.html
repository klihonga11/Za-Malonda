7.1 Introduction

The statement of financial position is a financial statement that shows the assets and liabilities of a business at a specific date and how the business has been financed.

This statement is prepared to understand the financial health of the business.

For example, it can tell a business owner whether the business owes more money to suppliers than what it actually has in the bank.

The numbers used in a statement of financial position are taken from the trial balance.

In the statement of financial position, the total of the assets must always be equal to the total of the liabilities plus the equity. This is called the accounting equation.

Assets = Liabilities + Equity
