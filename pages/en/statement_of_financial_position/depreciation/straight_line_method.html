7.4.2 Straight Line Method

The straight line method aims to spread the cost of a non-current asset by the same amount over its useful life.

This is the simplest and most common method of calculating depreciation.

Depreciation Charge = (Cost - Residual Value) / useful life

Depreciation charge is the amount added to the statement of profit or loss to spread the cost of non-current assets over the life of those assets.

Residual value is the amount a non-current asset is worth at the end of its useful life.


The statement of financial position records non-current assets as having a cost, accumulated depreciation and net book value.

Accumulated depreciation (Accum. Depn.) is the total depreciation that has been charged on an asset since it was bought.

Net book value (NBV) is calculated by taking the cost of a non-current asset and subtracting the accumulated depreciation that has been charged on that asset since it was bought.

Net Book Value (NBV) = Cost - Accumulated Depreciation


Thomas expects to use the bucket he bought at K 1,000 for two years, after which he will throw it away and buy a new bucket. Therefore, the cost is K 1,000, the residual value is K 0 and the useful life is two years. Using the formula above, the depreciation charge at the end of the first fiscal period will be K 500.

We will now include the K 500 in a new 'depreciation' account (an expense) and it will be debited as it is an increase. Since we are using double entry bookkeeping, we will also include the K 500 in the 'bucket' account (an asset) and it will be credited as it is a decrease.

The trial balace will need to updated with a depreciation account balance of K 500 and the bucket account balance will reduce by K 500.

In the statement of profit or loss, the depreciation charge of K 500 will now be included as an expense. This will reduce the net profit to K 5,500.

Lastly, in the statement of financial position, the K 500 would be the accumulated depreciation for the bucket. This would be subtracted from the cost of K 1000 to give a NBV of K 500. The net profit balance under Equity will also be updated to K 5,500.

For the next fiscal period, the process will be the same with the accumulated depreciation rising to K 1000, for a net book value of K 0 after which Thomas will throw away the bucket as planned.
