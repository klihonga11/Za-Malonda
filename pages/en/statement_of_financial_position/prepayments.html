7.7 Prepayments

A prepayment happens where an expense has been paid for but the benefit will only be received in the next fiscal period.

Since the prepayment will make the business money in the next fiscal period, a prepayment is a current asset.

For example, if Thomas pays the rent for his shop three months in advance going into the next fiscal period.

Since we are preparing financial statements for the fiscal period that just ended, we need to make changes to show that the benefit of the expense will only be received in the next fiscal period.

Therefore, in order to adjust the financial statements for a prepayment:

  • Subtract the prepaid amount from the expense on the statement of profit or loss.
  • Add the prepaid amount as a current asset on the statement of financial position. The prepayment should be listed before cash.
