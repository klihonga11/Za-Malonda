7.3.4.3 Reducing Balance Method

Using the reducing balance method, the depreciation charge is highest in the first year and then reduces every year after that.

This is done by calculating the depreciation percentage on the NBV of the asset.

Depreciation Charge = Net Book Value * Depreciation Percentage

Use this method of depreciation if the non-current asset is going to be used more at the beginning of its useful life than towards the end.


For example, Thomas wants to depreciate the bicycle he bought for K 15,000 at a rate of 20% per annum (every year) using the reducing balance method.

Using the formula, the first year's depreciation charge will be K 3,000. In the first year, the cost equals the NBV since the asset has never been depreciated.

The adjustments to the accounts, trial balance, and financial statements will follow the same process as the straight line method.

For the second year, the depreciation charge will be K 2,400 because the percentage is calculated on the NBV.

Similarly, in the third year, the depreciation charge will be K 1,920.

Businesses need to decide which depreciation method to use for each type of non-current asset. Once decided, businesses need to apply this policy consistently for all similar assets.
