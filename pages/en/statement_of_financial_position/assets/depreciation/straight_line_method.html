7.3.4.2 Straight Line Method

The straight line method aims to spread the cost of a non-current asset by the same amount over its useful life.

This is the simplest and most common method of calculating depreciation.

The depreciation charge is how much a non-current asset will reduce in value per fiscal period throughout its useful life.

Depreciation Charge = (Cost - Residual Value) / Useful Life

Cost is the amount of money the non-current asset was bought for.

Residual value is the amount a non-current asset is worth at the end of its useful life.

Useful life is how long a non-current asset will be used for.

The depreciation charge is added to the statement of profit or loss as an expense.


The statement of financial position records non-current assets as having a cost, accumulated depreciation, and net book value.

Accumulated depreciation (Accum. Depn.) is the total depreciation that has been charged on an asset since it was bought.

Net book value (NBV) is calculated by taking the cost of a non-current asset and subtracting the accumulated depreciation that has been charged on that asset since it was bought.

Net Book Value (NBV) = Cost - Accumulated Depreciation


For example, Thomas expects to use the bucket he bought at K 1,000 for two years, after which he will sell it for K200. Therefore, the cost is K 1,000, the residual value is K 200 and the useful life is two years. Using the formula above, the depreciation charge at the end of the first fiscal period will be K 400.

Since this depreciation charge is an expense, we will need to record the transaction and update the accounts.

We will now include the K 400 in a new 'depreciation' account (an expense) and it will be debited as it is an increase. Since we are using double entry bookkeeping, we will also include the K 400 in the 'bucket' account (an asset) and it will be credited as it is a decrease.

The trial balance will need to be updated with a depreciation account balance of K 400 and the bucket account balance will reduce by K 400.

In the statement of profit or loss, the depreciation charge of K 400 will now be included as an expense. This will reduce the net profit to K 5,600.

Lastly, in the statement of financial position, the K 400 would be the accumulated depreciation for the bucket. This would be subtracted from the cost of K 1000 to give an NBV of K 600. The net profit balance under Equity will also be updated to K 5,600.

For the next fiscal period, the process will be the same with the accumulated depreciation rising to K 800, for a net book value of K 200 after which Thomas will sell the bucket as planned.
