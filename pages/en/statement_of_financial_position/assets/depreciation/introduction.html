7.3.4.1 Introduction

When a business buys a non-current asset, it is expected to benefit the business over a number of fiscal periods.

However, with the exception of land, this non-current asset will not last forever.

Depreciation is a method used to calculate the cost of a non-current asset as it reduces in value over the fiscal periods in which it will benefit the business. There are two methods of calculating this cost:

  • Straight Line Method
  • Reducing Balance Method
