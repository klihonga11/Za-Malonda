5.1 Introduction

A trial balance is a list of all the account balances (from the general ledger) at a specific point in time.

This point in time is usually at the end of a business’s fiscal period.

A fiscal period is a period where the business operates at the end of which, the business’s accounts are closed.

A fiscal period is usually a year (twelve months) but it can also be a quarter (four months).

A trial balance lists all the account balances, showing whether there is a debit or a credit balance on each account.

The trial balance should always balance – the total of the debit balances should equal the total of the credit balances.

If it does not balance, it means there is a mistake in your general ledger or journal and you need to correct it before preparing any financial statements.
