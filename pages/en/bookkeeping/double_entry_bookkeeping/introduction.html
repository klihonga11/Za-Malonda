2.3.1 Introduction

Double entry bookkeeping is the recommended and most widely used form of bookkeeping.

It is the most widely used because it has many advantages over single entry bookeeping such as automatic error checking.

When recording a transaction, at least two entries are made for each transaction – a debit in one account and a credit in another.

An account is where you record the increases and decreases in money for a single item.

For example, Thomas paying for transport will be recorded in a ‘transport’ account. Any other time Thomas uses money for transport, it will also be recorded in this account.

A debit is an entry into an account that is recorded on the left side of the account.

A credit is an entry into an account that is recorded on the right side of the account.


Note: Don't worry about why a debit is on the left and a credit is on the right. There is no secret meaning behind it. It has just always been done that way. You just need to memorize it.
