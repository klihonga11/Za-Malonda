2.3.3 Managing Accounts

The first three types of accounts (Drawings, Expenses and Assets) are to be debited when their amounts increase, and credited when their amounts decrease.

The last three accounts (Liabilities, Equity, Revenue) are to be credited when their amounts increase, and debited when their amounts decrease.

One way to remember this is to memorize it as “DC DEALER”.

DC stands for Debit-Credit. Debit on the left and credit on the right.

For the first three letters on the left, DEA (Drawings, Expenses, Assets), to increase the account, you must debit which is an entry on the left side of the account.

For the last three letters on the right, LER (Liabilities, Equity, Revenue), to increase the account, you must credit which is an entry on the right side of the account.


Note: Don't worry about why an increase or decrease is a certain type of account will mean a debit or a credit. There is no secret meaning behind it. It has just always been done that way. You just need to memorize it.
