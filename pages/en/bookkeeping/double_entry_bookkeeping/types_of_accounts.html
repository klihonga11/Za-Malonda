2.3.2 Types of Accounts

There are six types of accounts:

1. Drawings – money taken out of the business by the owner for personal use e.g. for school fees.

2. Expenses – money used by the business to operate on a day-to-day basis e.g. water and electricity, transport, rent, etc.

3. Assets – items owned by the business e.g. a plastic bucket.

4. Liabilities – money owed by the business e.g. a loan.

5. Equity – money put into the business by the owner e.g. capital.

6. Revenue (also known as Income) – money earned by the business e.g. sales.
