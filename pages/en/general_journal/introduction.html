3.1 Introduction

This is the first step of the double entry bookkeeping process.

This is a book where all financial transactions are first recorded.

The transactions are recorded according to the date from the earliest to the latest date.

The process of entering transactions into a journal is known as journalizing.

A journal is also known as a daybook.

For a small business, simply having a general journal is enough. But as the number of transactions increases, a business may want to have special journals to record different transactions such as:

  • Sales Journal – used to record credit sales.
  • Purchases Journal – used to record credit purchases.
  • Cash Receipts Journal – used to record any transaction where any money comes into the business including cash sales.
  • Cash Payments Journal – used to record any transaction where any money goes out of the business including cash purchases.

Any other transactions that cannot be entered in these four special journals will be entered in the general journal. For example, if someone returns goods that are expired or not working.


Note: For purposes of this course, we will only record entries in the general journal.
