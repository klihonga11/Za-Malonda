6.4 Net Profit

The second part of the statement of profit or loss is for calculating the net profit.

Net profit is the profit a business makes after removing the cost of the products it sold and any costs it takes to run the business on a day-to-day basis.

Net profit = gross profit - total expenses


Thomas spends K 500 for transport which is an expense to his business. His gross profit is K 2,500, therefore his net profit on 31 January 2020 is K 2,000.

When talking about whether a business is profitable, it refers to the net profit and not the gross profit.

If a business makes a loss, this means that the business has more costs than it does sales.

When a loss happens, the net profit will be negative.
