6.7 Breakeven Point

When you start your business, you will be operating at a loss. You will have spent money on your inventory without making any sales.

As you start to make sales, you will begin getting closer to making a profit. But, you might still make a loss because the number of products that you sold did not bring in enough money to pay for your expenses.

The breakeven point is the number of products you need to sell to neither make a profit or a loss.

It is the point where revenue income equals revenue expenditure and the net profit equals 0.

Selling one more product at this point will result in making a profit while selling one less product will result in a loss.

Thomas bought sugarcane at K 100 and sells it at K 150. He needed to pay K 500 in transport to buy the sugarcane. What is his breakeven point?

Thomas needs to sell ten sugarcane in order to breakeven.

If he sells nine sugarcane, he will make K 450 in sales. His expenses will be K 500. Therefore, he will make a loss of K 50.

If he sells eleven sugarcane, he will make K 550 in sales. His expenses will be K 500. Therefore, he will make a profit of K 50.

The breakeven point is very important because it gives you a sales target to aim for in order to start being profitable.
