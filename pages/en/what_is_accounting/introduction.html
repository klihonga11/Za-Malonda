1.1 Introduction

Accounting is the recording and communicating of financial transactions of a business for both the people running the business itself (internal users) and those outside the business (external users).

A financial transaction is an event that involves the business where money is exchanged. A business paying salaries to its employees is a financial transaction but a business reaching a hundred likes on their Facebook page is not.

Internal users, like managers, can use this accounting information to make business decisions. For example, calculating how much to charge for a product.

External users, like banks or investors, can use this information to decide if it is worth working with the business. For example, an investor needs to know if a business is going to become profitable before investing.

Overall, the reason accounting is important is that it tells you how well your business is doing.
